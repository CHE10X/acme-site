# Welcome to ACME

You're running agents. At some point — usually the wrong moment — one of them stops working in a way you won't catch until it's too late.

ACME is the layer that tells you before that happens.

## The Problem

Standard monitoring catches crashes. But agents fail quietly:

- **Silent failures** — the agent looks healthy but stopped making progress hours ago
- **Stalls** — it's waiting on something that will never come, and it won't tell you
- **Drift** — behavior shifts session-to-session in ways that are hard to pin down

These don't trigger alerts. They just silently fail. And you find out when something downstream breaks, or a user complains, or you check in and realize it's been dead for hours.

## Who This Is For

If you're running a handful of agents — for a side project, a home automation setup, or a small business you're building yourself — you're the person ACME was built for.

You probably don't have an ops team. You are the ops team. When something breaks, it's just you, a terminal, and whatever logs you have. ACME gives you better visibility before things break, and better tools for when they do.

The free tools are genuinely useful on their own — not crippled demos. They're how you find out what ACME is before committing to anything.
